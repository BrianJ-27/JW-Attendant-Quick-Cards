JW Attendant Quick Guides

Always refer to our congregation's S-288 security plan document for detailed protocols.
This does not replace it.

1: General Guidelines (All Attendants)

Quick-action protocols for immediate response.

Demeanor & Timing:

Be warm, friendly, and kind to all attendees (Col. 4:6). Be at your assigned post 30 minutes before the meeting starts.

Violent Individual Protocol:

  • Spotting: If a real threat is seen and confirmed, the Auditorium Attendant calls 911 immediately.
  • Lock & Barricade: Secure all doors immediately to prevent entry.
  • Alert: The Auditorium Attendant alerts the congregation of the risk.
  • Evacuate: If the hall is not surrounded, direct attendees away from the violence.
  • Subdue: If an attacker enters, multiple brothers work together to subdue them.
  • Shelter: If trapped, hide in a locked room, block the door quietly, and stay out of sight.

Medical Emergencies:

Immediately inform another brother and go to the scene, requesting assistance from any medical professionals present. Ensure 911 is called by the auditorium attendant if needed; the speaker may be advised to pause the program.

Disruptions:

If a disruptive person refuses to leave, state: “Your implied invitation has been rescinded” and call the police.

Sheltering:

These shelter-in-place steps also apply to civil disturbances, severe weather, or environmental emergencies.

2: Role-Specific Key Points

Individual duties for a coordinated response.

Entrance Attendant

Locking: Secure doors 5 mins after start; reopen after concluding prayer.

Screening: Verify intentions of unknown persons; deny entry to aggressive or intoxicated individuals.

Walk Around Inspection:

  • Grab a second trained security brother to conduct one staggered, unannounced inspection of the building and lot.
  • Verify all exterior doors are locked. Use flashlights at night; do not wear high-visibility vests.

Evacuation: Lead the 2nd school, left, and center sections to the West Field.

Auditorium Attendant

Congregation Management: Alert the friends of threats and coordinate their movement.

Emergency Liaison: Serve as the primary point of contact for emergency responders.

Police Contact: Primary brother responsible for calling 911/police during disruptions or security threats.

Evacuation Plan:

  • Lead the sound booth side to the West Field.
  • Perform a final sweep of all rooms (restrooms/lobby) to ensure the building is empty.
  • Conduct a headcount in the field and compare it to the in-person attendance.

Security Camera Attendant

Monitoring: Maintain constant contact with the Entrance Attendant regarding parking lot activity.

Call Backup: If the Auditorium Attendant is unable to call 911, make the call and immediately inform the Auditorium Attendant.

Walkarounds: Maintain increased vigilance and monitoring while the building/lot inspection is underway.

Prioritize safety and remain calm. Thank you for your dedication and service. ~Hebrews 6:10
